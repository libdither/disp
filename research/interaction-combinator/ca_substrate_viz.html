what this shows

finite state per cell

The simulation state IS a grid of cells now, nothing else. Each cell holds at most: one agent (a ~4-bit tag, one direction per port, a sleep bit) and a small stack of wire segments (each: two directions, a 2-bit class, a flow direction). No ids, no global wire objects, no routed paths: a wire is whatever chain of segments physically connects two ports, and following it is an act (the engine's chain-typing pulse after each splice is the one instant shortcut, standing in for a signal that would really take a tick per cell). Rewrites only touch the two dying cells, their adjacent stubs, and a few neighboring free cells (the created-short lemma made mechanical); a splice is the dying cell becoming wire; slack is your old cell becoming wire for the connections you dragged. Click any cell to pin its state and hover the underlined fields for what each component means.

sequential vs parallel, this term

work is schedule-invariant (strong confluence); ticks (span) is what parallelism and migration move; transport varies with the realized motion (every walk and migration step is priced).

honest approximations

Still engine conveniences rather than local rules: the parallel tick commits through a global arbiter (no Margolus handshake); chain re-typing after a splice is instant (really a pulse, one cell per tick); rewrite-site cell claiming spirals outward (really pressure-mediated); wire layers per cell are unbounded but counted (real cells get 2-3; watch the layers readout); pressure is jam-triggered repulsion, not a diffusing field; no wire-RC death-pulse cancellation (demand_schedule_viz.html shows that mechanism).
