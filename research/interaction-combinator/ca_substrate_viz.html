what this shows

the substrate rules

Agents occupy cells. A wire is a routed chain of cells (your intuition: chains of forwarder agents, the ι of the embedding theorem). Nothing acts at a distance: a redex may fire only when the two principals sit in adjacent cells, so a pending redex first walks: the producer re-materializes one cell along the chain per step (one ι-elimination = one unit of transport, watch the yellow-tinted cells slide). A move drags the mover's other wires one cell longer (drift is charged: the ledger's insertion rule, visible as trailing slack). Two wires may share a cell (a via, drawn as a diamond: the second layer); wires may also pass under agent cells. Sequential = one event per tick (a pointer machine's serial order). Parallel = every enabled event fires in the same tick (the CA's synchronous step): same total work, far fewer ticks wherever the net is wide.

sequential vs parallel, this term

work is schedule-invariant (strong confluence: same interactions, same normal form, either mode). transport wobbles a little between modes: blocked movers take congestion detours, and which cells are congested depends on timing (the embedding is dynamic even though the computation is not). ticks (the span, the wall-clock proxy) is what collapses: the ratio is the free parallelism the single-principal-port invariant hands the substrate.

honest approximations

This toy differs from the real design (SPATIAL_IC.md §13, the E4 simulator) in: parallel ticks commit through a global arbiter rather than a Margolus block handshake; straightening is chain-local re-lay rather than per-cell retraction; pressure exists only in minimal form (jam sites repel neighbors for a tick, plus a density cap and a bounded stall grace) rather than a diffusing field with vacancy motion; wire layers under agents are unlimited (real cells get 2-3 plus vias); no wire-RC death pulse (see demand_schedule_viz.html for that mechanism at the net level). Migration is the §2.3 mechanism: per-tick Metropolis drift over the force of each agent's wire first-hop directions (weights hot 12 / principal 2 / plumbing 1; redex participants reel in only and HOLD at contact; sleeping agents are clock-gated), hash(agent,tick) PRNG so runs stay bit-reproducible. Transport counts BOTH delivery steps and migration moves (the embedding theorem's definition): migration spends transport to buy span, and the four knobs it took to make every example complete (weight dominance, hold-at-contact, jam pressure, density cap) are a live preview of the §13 knob-sweep and the §14.1 liveness question.
