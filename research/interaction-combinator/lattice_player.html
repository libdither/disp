RUNG 2 REPLAY — THE RUST LATTICE, FRAME BY FRAME

A replay client over rust-ca-lattice traces (the engine is the model; this page only draws). Each frame is one TICK of the rung-2 sequential schedule: fire every enabled adjacent facing pair (rescanning after each — a fire is one footprint-atomic transition), then give every eligible producer one reel step along its principal wire. Deterministic: cells are scanned in coordinate order, so the same term always produces this exact movie. Regenerate or add traces with cargo run --release --bin dump-run <name> and drop the JSON here.

WHAT THIS SHOWS (AND DOES NOT)

Correctness is schedule-independent by construction here: every transition declares its cell footprint and the engine asserts writes stay inside it, and every transition preserves the projection onto the shadow interaction net (checked each tick in these traces). What this rung does NOT yet have: parallel schedules (Margolus / async fuzz — rung 3 runs THESE SAME transitions in other orders and must produce the same normal form) and liveness policies (no straighten, drift, or pressure yet — which is why the ksimple, share2 and chain3 traces end STUCK in congested pockets: the honest §13 residue, LOCAL_CA_DESIGN.md).
