RUNG 2 REPLAY — THE LIFTED LATTICE, FRAME BY FRAME

A replay client over rust-ca-lattice traces (the engine is the model; this page only draws). Cells live at (x,y,z): bilayer traces have two planes (the 2.5D chip), full3d traces as many as the run used — upper planes draw offset up-right and dimmer. There are no vias and no tucks: a crossing is two wires at different z. Each frame is one tick: fire every enabled adjacent facing pair, slide one blocking strand out of each stuck walker's way (the excluded-volume move), reel every eligible producer one cell, then retract U-turn slack to fixpoint. Deterministic: the same term always produces this exact movie. Regenerate or add traces with cargo run --release --bin dump-run <name> [ticks] [bilayer|full3d] and drop the JSON here.

WHAT THIS SHOWS (AND DOES NOT)

Correctness is schedule-independent by construction: every transition declares its cell footprint (writes machine-checked inside it) and preserves the projection onto the shadow interaction net. Liveness is measured, never assumed: the bare sequential schedule still has no fields, and bilayer runs wedge at fire seams where splices and trails exhaust the single overflow plane (several embedded traces end STUCK — the honest LOCAL_CA_DESIGN.md §13 residue the pressure field addresses next). full3d runs of the same terms complete.
