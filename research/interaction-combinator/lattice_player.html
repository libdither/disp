LIFTED LATTICE REPLAY — FRAME BY FRAME

model + trace semantics
A replay client over rust-ca-lattice traces (the engine is the model; this page only draws). Cells live at (x,y,z): bilayer traces have two planes (the 2.5D chip), while full3d traces use signed depth as needed. Positive z draws up-right, negative z down-left, and nonzero planes dim with depth. There are no vias or tucks. Routed crossings can separate in z; a switchbox cell may also hold up to three disjoint paired strands. The strict local-motion kernel is a staged, radius-one handshake: a cell reads itself and its six face-neighbors from one frozen local-motion snapshot, then writes only itself. A calm, ψ-demanded, or χ-descending producer of arity 1–3 advances by a local star: its principal enters the next cell. For arity 1 the vacated cell clears; for arity 2 its auxiliary bends through that cell; for arity 3 one auxiliary takes that back bend and the other locally extends or truncates through a swept square. Roles commit only after their neighboring roles agree. The endpoint agent and its intermediate forwarder therefore commit as one translation: the agent occupies the forwarder's cell and the principal edge loses that wire segment. No wire or agent pushes a foreign wire. Blockage pumps χ; χ is the sole clearance license, and even then each occupant reshapes or moves only itself (including the local straight-wire bulge). A calm shared principal switchbox waits through a bounded local persistence mark before emitting low pressure, so a transient crossing does not immediately bloom. ψ carries demand, the tension survey identifies removable bends, and retract cashes those bends into shorter wire. Once the shadow net reaches semantic NF, the trace may continue through a settling tail so geometric contraction remains visible. Deterministic: the same term, topology, engine revision, and options produce the same movie. The bundled traces contain one exact snapshot for every evaluator tick, including the post-NF settling tail. Optional compact exports may use a stride greater than one; those keep structural frames and merge skipped events into the next recorded state. Playback advances recorded states at the selected visual frame rate; the HUD retains each state's exact simulation tick and reports any sampled tick gap. Regenerate or add traces with cargo run --release --bin dump-run <name> [ticks] [bilayer|full3d] [stride=1] and drop the JSON here.

AGENTS & PORTS

Port 0 is always the principal (▸): producers emit their value there, consumers consume there, and a fire needs the two principals facing across one edge. Aux ports carry everything else. On the canvas a white triangle marks the principal face, a hollow circle marks aux 1, and a diamond marks aux 2. Producers use four-sided diamond bodies; consumers use six-sided faceted diamonds.

WHAT THIS SHOWS (AND DOES NOT)

Projection preservation is checked against the shadow interaction net. Each update in the strict local-motion kernel takes a frozen center-plus-six-neighbor view and emits only the center's next state; those updates neither inspect a diagonal nor mutate a neighbor atomically. Fire placement, demanded reel, χ-licensed agent movement, flip, slide, retract, and grow are host-planned bounded-footprint parts of this evaluator; they are not included in this locality claim. Liveness is measured, never assumed. The local protocol reads bonded ψ demand and Jacobi-relaxed χ pressure; the tension survey remains a separate host scheduler phase. Star roles and pressure bulges are multi-tick commitments because a diagonal swept square is not itself a neighbor; information crosses it as two face-local messages. The L/C/S readout separates wire length L from endpoint chord lower bound C; S=L−C is candidate geometric slack. Semantic NF and geometric settling are deliberately separate: a correct normal form can still have residual S to contract. STUCK or capped traces remain measurements of liveness, not claims that liveness follows from projection preservation.
